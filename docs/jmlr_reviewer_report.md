# JMLR-style reviewer report: marginSVM

## Recommendation

**Major revision; not ready for acceptance in its present form.** The method is
technically substantial, the C++ implementation is useful, and the complete
simulation matrix is stronger than the earlier evaluation. The main empirical
claim is credible within the authors' simulator: marginSVM has the best mean rank
and positive paired effects against all included direct label refiners. However,
the paper does not yet meet the standard of a principled machine-learning paper
with broad relevance. The learning task, algorithmic novelty, hyperparameter
development, statistical units, and computational claims require sharper treatment.

I used the official JMLR reviewer questions on goals, novelty, soundness, clarity,
and reproducibility, together with the journal's requirement that an application
paper expose a broader machine-learning contribution. See the
[JMLR reviewer guide](https://www.jmlr.org/reviewer-guide.html) and
[author information](https://www.jmlr.org/author-info.html).

## Major comments

1. **The learning problem and assumptions are not formalized.** The paper starts
   from coordinates and corrupted labels, but it never states a statistical task
   that separates recoverable pointwise noise from non-identifiable coherent
   relabelling. Define the latent labeling problem, the assumed local regularity,
   the corruption model, and the role of tissue identifiers. Explain which claims
   are estimable from these inputs and which are impossible.

2. **The novelty relative to localized SVMs and the authors' prior spatial SVM is
   insufficiently isolated.** The manuscript lists components but does not show
   which are new, which are standard, and which are responsible for the gain.
   Add algorithmic pseudocode, a contribution table, and an ablation that removes
   cross-fitting, adaptive tiles, overlap, graph fusion, and TV decoding. Because
   the authors have prior KODAMA work, the submission must state the substantive
   delta precisely.

3. **One corruption fraction is used throughout the complete geometric matrix.**
   A method intended for refinement should be tested from light contamination,
   where unnecessary changes dominate risk, through severe contamination, where
   local evidence degrades. The colorectal test varies the fraction, but that does
   not replace controlled synthetic experiments. Repeat representative uniform
   and extreme-density conditions at multiple corruption fractions.

4. **The statistical analysis partly treats generated cases as independent
   datasets.** Averaging three seeds within 236 conditions is an improvement, but
   all conditions are generated by closely related code and many share the same
   geometric template. The paper should identify the estimand, use condition-level
   paired inference, report wins and losses rather than only a pooled mean, and
   avoid interpreting very small p-values as external generalization.

5. **Rare-region behavior weakens the 'first choice' framing.** SpaGCN has
   substantially higher sparsest-region and worst-class recall. This is not a
   secondary metric in spatial transcriptomics: thin ducts, vessels, and immune
   niches may be precisely the regions of interest. Report the failure mode by
   concentration and error type, describe whether topology protection helps, and
   make the method's operating recommendation conditional on rare-region priority.

6. **Hyperparameter selection and the trust-field extension need a clearer audit
   trail.** The default contains many constants, while the opt-in extension improves
   colorectal data but loses broadly in simulation. State which data selected each
   setting, provide sensitivity or ablation results, and avoid presenting the
   extension as a general improvement. A clean default/experimental distinction
   should appear in the abstract, methods, and software interface.

7. **Comparator validity is plausible but not fully demonstrated.** Restricting
   comparisons to methods that consume coordinates and labels is appropriate.
   Nevertheless, the paper should give exact update rules and neighborhood sizes,
   cite the source locations, and test that the C++ implementations reproduce the
   published R/Python rules on small examples. End-to-end spatial clustering should
   remain excluded, but this exclusion needs a formal input-equivalence argument.

8. **The systems section lacks a complexity analysis and mixes implemented and
   aspirational backends.** Give time and memory complexity in terms of points,
   tile multiplicity, landmarks, classes, graph degree, and TV iterations. Report
   CPU scaling and peak memory beyond the 6,000-point benchmark. CUDA and Metal
   should be described only as provider interfaces until native kernels are tested.

9. **Biological validation is insufficient for an application claim.** Injected
   corruption of one colorectal annotation measures recovery of that annotation,
   not biological correctness. Independent tissues, platforms, and expert or
   marker-based endpoints are absent. This cannot be repaired with more simulation;
   the paper must either narrow its claim to algorithm/software validation or add
   independent real-data evidence before making a spatial-transcriptomics methods
   claim.

## Minor comments

1. Use `marginSVM` consistently; remove the obsolete name "Structured Spatial SVM."
2. Call softmax output "evidence" or "score" unless calibration is demonstrated.
3. Clarify that the displayed 3D atlas is a projection of a three-coordinate fit.
4. State whether multiple tissues are independent simulations or repeated geometry
   with isolated graph construction.
5. Provide exact seeds, software versions, raw per-scenario metrics, and one command
   that regenerates every principal table and figure.
6. The abstract should distinguish the default from the experimental trust-field
   extension without devoting disproportionate space to the latter.

## Missing experiments required for revision

1. Current marginSVM across 5%, 15%, 25%, and 40% corruption under both uniform
   and extreme concentration, all four corruption mechanisms, and all geometries.
2. Component ablation and parameter sensitivity on seeds excluded from final tests.
3. CPU runtime and peak-memory scaling through at least 50,000 observations, with a
   larger stress point if feasible.
4. Explicit sparse-region analysis, including conditions in which marginSVM loses.
5. Small-case equivalence tests for SpaGCN and GraphST refinement implementations.
6. Independent biological datasets before claims of biological correction; this
   item may remain an acknowledged blocker until those datasets are available.
